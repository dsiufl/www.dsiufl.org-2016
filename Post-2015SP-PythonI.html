DataSci UF's introduction to programming with Python. A very practical, high level language, Python is used extensively in the machine learning, neural network, and data science communities. We will be covering basic operations, data sets, and syntax, and by the end of the course, participants will have a solid grasp of Python's fundamentals.

Our next session is Tuesday 3/24/2015 at 5:10pm - 7:20pm in Turlington 2319

We will be providing free food (Publix platters with subs, veggies, etc.), so come join us for a fun, interactive learning experience.

Before arriving please:

Prepare your computer by installing our virtual machine and software. The entire process should take between 30 minutes and an hour, depending on your internet speed.

1. Start downloading the virtual machine at: http://python.acis.ufl.edu/datasci-uf-student.ova.zip

2. If you don’t have VirtualBox, download and install it here.

3. Extract the .ova file to your desktop or other convenient location.

4. Double-click the .ova file or open it with VirtualBox, and click “import”.

5. After the import is finished, click “start”, and wait for the machine to load (this may take a few moments).

6. Enter “gogators” when requested for the password.

7. Once the desktop loads, a notification will appear in the taskbar asking you to update the software. Click “remind me later”.

8. Open the Readme and follow the short list of instructions provided to ensure everything is working properly.
